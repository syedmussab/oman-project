AUDIO DESIGN DOCUMENT

1. PROJECT OVERVIEW

Audio editing and effects for narrative voice-over about Omani culture. The audio clip demonstrates professional editing techniques with cleaned audio and multiple format support.

2. SOURCE MATERIAL

  • Voice-over: Original recording by student
  • Background Music: Royalty-free Omani-inspired ambient music
  • Ambient Sounds: Traditional Omani sounds (market, mosque)

3. AUDIO EDITING TECHNIQUES

1. Noise Reduction

Removed background hiss and electrical hum (50/60Hz).

2. Click/Pop Removal

Cleaned audio artefacts from recording equipment.

3. De-Esser

Reduced harsh sibilance in voice recording.

4. Volume Normalization

Matched overall volume levels across all clips.

5. EQ Adjustment

Enhanced voice clarity with proper equalization.

6. Compression

Applied dynamic range compression for consistency.

7. Fade In/Out

Added smooth fade effects at beginnings and ends.

8. Crossfade

Smooth transitions between audio segments.

6. DESIGN RATIONALE (250 words)

The audio production for this Oman Celebrities project demonstrates professional editing techniques essential for delivering high-quality educational content on the web.

Noise reduction was prioritized to remove background distractions, particularly the characteristic air conditioning hum common in indoor recordings. The 50Hz electrical noise was eliminated using spectral editing, while broadband hiss was reduced without compromising voice clarity.

Click and pop removal addresses common recording artefacts that detract from professional presentation. These micro-artefacts were removed using a combination of automatic detection and manual editing for optimal results.

Volume normalization ensures consistent playback across all listening environments, from high-end speakers to mobile devices. The target loudness of -16 LUFS (integrated) matches broadcast standards and ensures comfortable listening.

EQ adjustment enhances voice clarity by reducing muddiness in the low-mid frequencies and controlling harshness in the high frequencies. This processing preserves the natural voice character while improving intelligibility.

Multiple format support ensures accessibility across all browsers and devices. MP3 serves as the universal format, while OGG provides open-source support. WAV serves as the master for future re-processing, and AAC ensures quality on Apple devices.

The final output balances quality and file size, achieving professional broadcast quality at web-appropriate bitrates suitable for streaming and download.
