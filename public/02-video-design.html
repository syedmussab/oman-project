VIDEO DESIGN DOCUMENT

1. PROJECT OVERVIEW

A 1-minute video sequence showcasing Omani culture with effects, voice-over, and credits.

2. SOURCE MATERIAL

  • Original Video: Student-recorded footage of Omani locations
  • Stock Footage: Licensed from Pexels/Pixabay (royalty-free)
  • Voice-over: Original recording by student
  • Music: Royalty-free Omani-inspired music

3. VIDEO EFFECTS (Minimum 5 required)

1. Color Correction / Enhancement

White balance, saturation, and color grading adjustments to enhance Omani cultural colors (gold, blue).

2. 3D Effects

3D text overlays and animated transitions between scenes.

3. Morphing

Smooth scene transitions using morphing effects.

4. Animation Effects

Animated graphics overlaying video footage.

5. Text Effects

Animated titles and captions explaining cultural elements.

6. Time-based Effect

Progressive color change throughout video (implemented over time).

4. VOICE-OVER & CAPTIONS

  • Voice-over: Describes effects used throughout video
  • Captions: WebVTT format for HTML5 video
  • Credits: Sources of all material at end

5. OUTPUT FORMATS

5.1 SDR Upload (YouTube)

  • Format: MP4 (H.264)
  • Resolution: 1920x1080
  • Frame Rate: 30fps
  • Bitrate: 8Mbps
  • Audio: AAC 192kbps

5.2 HDR Upload (YouTube)

  • Format: MP4 (H.265/HEVC)
  • Resolution: 3840x2160 (4K)
  • Frame Rate: 60fps
  • Bitrate: 20-53Mbps
  • Color: Rec.2020

5.3 HTML5 Native

  • MP4 (H.264) - Universal browser support
  • WebM (VP9) - Chrome/Firefox
  • Ogg (Theora) - Firefox legacy

6. DESIGN RATIONALE (250 words)

The video production for this Oman Celebrities project was designed to create an engaging, educational experience that showcases Omani culture through modern editing techniques.

The time-based color effect was implemented progressively to maintain viewer engagement throughout the 1-minute duration while reinforcing the cultural color palette (gold, blue, and white). This aligns with the project's educational objectives by creating visual continuity.

Color correction was prioritized to ensure authentic representation of Omani architectural elements and traditional dress. The warm color grading enhances the golden desert tones while maintaining the characteristic blue of Omani architecture.

Multiple output formats ensure accessibility across all platforms. The SDR format serves general web viewing, while HDR provides an enhanced experience for capable displays. HTML5 native formats ensure broad browser compatibility without requiring plugins.

The voice-over serves dual purposes: providing narration and documenting the editing techniques used. This meta-educational approach helps viewers understand the production process while enjoying the content.

Credits are essential for educational integrity, acknowledging all sources of material and ensuring proper attribution for any third-party content used in the production.
